Call to action

Move from awareness to meaningful support

Low income is connected to housing, health, education, and food security. Supporting wellbeing means supporting the systems that make wellbeing possible— and prioritizing Indigenous-led leadership and solutions.

1) Participate

Join the Cold Water Challenge as an awareness action—pair it with facts and learning, not spectacle.

Campaign details

2) Share responsibly

Share this site and one statistic with context. Avoid blaming individuals; explain systemic causes.

3) Learn from Indigenous-led sources

Choose Indigenous-led educators, organizations, and community voices. Learning is ongoing.

When possible, use sources from the Nation/region you live on.

Support Indigenous-led community initiatives

What “support” can look like

  • Donate to Indigenous-led organizations working on housing, food sovereignty, health, or youth programs.
  • Attend community-led events (with respect, consent, and listening).
  • Advocate for equitable funding and accountability in services that affect quality of life.
  • Share opportunities and reduce barriers where you have influence.

Keep the message clear

We support multidimensional wellbeing—not “fixing” people.
Income is one part of a bigger picture: housing, infrastructure, education access, culturally safe health care, and food systems.

If you’re unsure what’s appropriate, prioritize: listen → learn → follow Indigenous leadership.

A short script you can use

“Low income in First Nations communities is shaped by colonial systems and ongoing inequities. When income is limited, choices for safe housing, health care access, education supports, and healthy food shrink. I’m learning from Indigenous-led sources and supporting Indigenous-led initiatives.”
