Did You Know?

Surprising facts that challenge common myths

Flip the cards. Then try the quiz. The goal is reflection—understanding that low income is systemic, not a personal flaw.

Flip cards (tap to reveal)

Quick quiz (choose one answer per question)

1) Which statement best explains low income in many First Nations communities?
Reflection: Systemic barriers (policy, underinvestment, access, discrimination) shape outcomes across generations. This is not about blaming individuals.
2) In 2021, what share of Indigenous people lived in low-income households?
Answer: About 18.8% (Statistics Canada, 2021 Census).
3) Which is an example of how income affects education?
Reflection: Learning needs conditions—safe space, access, stability. Low income restricts those conditions.
4) True or false: Food insecurity only happens to people below the poverty line.
Answer: False. Food insecurity can occur above the poverty line due to high costs and constrained budgets.
