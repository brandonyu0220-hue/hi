Income inequality reduces quality of life in many First Nations communities.

Low income is not an individual failure. In Canada, it is shaped by historical and ongoing colonial systems that limited economic participation and created long-term barriers to housing, health care, education, and food security.

A few facts to start

Low income (2021)

In 2021, 18.8% of Indigenous people lived in a low-income household, compared with 10.7% of the non-Indigenous population. (Statistics Canada, 2021 Census)

Housing crowding (2021)

In 2021, 17.1% of Indigenous people lived in crowded housing‚Äîabout double the non-Indigenous rate (9.4%). (Statistics Canada, 2021 Census)

Why this matters

Income affects basic needs‚Äîsafe housing, transportation, school supplies, internet, healthy food, and access to care. When systems restrict resources, quality of life drops.
